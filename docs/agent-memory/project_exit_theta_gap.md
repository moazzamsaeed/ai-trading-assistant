**FOLLOW-UP (revisit after the paper week): directional 0DTE longs DO have a hard stop, but it's DEEP (−50% in aggressive mode); the gap is the absence of a TIGHTER theta/time backstop between the thesis-exit and that −50% floor.** (CORRECTED 2026-06-23 — an earlier version of this note wrongly said "no dollar stop at all".) The downside exits, whichever hits first:
- **−50% hard stop** (`executor.py:45` `_EXIT_PCT`): aggressive mode sl=0.50 → `stop_premium = fill × (1 − 0.50)`; selective sl=0.30. `.env` = `DIRECTIONAL_MODE=aggressive` → −50%. The stop_premium starts here and ratchets UP via the trailing logic as the trade profits (confirmed: #80 old_stop 0.59 = 1.18×0.5 → ratcheted to 1.432 at +21%).
- `zdte_early_cut` (`zdte_early_loss_cut_pct=0.25`): loss ≤ −25% **AND** `vwap_broken`. Loss alone is NOT enough — price must also break VWAP.
- `thesis_invalidated`: `pnl < 0` AND ≥2 confirmed reversal signals (momentum flip).
- trailing stop: only engages after a +25% peak.
The issue: the −50% floor is deep, and the other two need a THESIS break — so a stalled-but-not-reversed loser (price clinging to VWAP) can bleed a long way before any cut.

This is DELIBERATE — a prior dollar-stop approach bled −$5,235 by cutting correct-direction trades on intraday noise (comment in exit_monitor), so cuts are thesis-based to avoid whipsaw. **The tradeoff, observed live:** a 0DTE long whose thesis is technically intact (price clinging just above/below its VWAP) but that is bleeding pure THETA has no backstop — it rides until either price finally breaks VWAP (then the early-cut fires instantly if already past −25%) or the 15:50 ET force-close.

KEY: the loss DEEPENED from −29.5% → −44% in the ~34 min between crossing the loss threshold and the thesis actually breaking — the exact uncapped-theta-bleed this gap describes. Textbook −EV long-0DTE-call outcome the strategy review flagged. [[project_strategy_review_0618]] says this leg is proven −EV; this is WHY it bleeds even with "protections."
